## Depth-Supervised 3DGS for Novel View Synthesis (Kitchen/Fruit Scene)

### Context
Takara is working on generating novel view training data for robotics from walking videos. The dataset: `sage_batch_walking/0001_839920_seq_006_fruit/` — ~4 seconds of walking through a kitchen, 120 frames, using every 4th frame (30) for 3DGS training.

### What We Tried

| Approach | Result | Why It Failed |
|----------|--------|---------------|
| Basic 3DGS (7k iter, 30 frames) | ±5cm novel views max | Sparse views, narrow trajectory |
| Longer training (15k iter) | PSNR 26.6 (vs 25.9), same novel view limit | More iterations can't add missing geometry |
| Depth supervision (MASt3R inverse depth) | PSNR 20.1 — worse | Depth alignment issues, loss conflict |
| Diffusion inpainting/img2img | Hallucinated kitchens | Doesn't preserve ground truth |
| ControlNet + depth | Right geometry, wrong appearance | No scene-specific knowledge |
| IP-Adapter | Right colors, distorted details | Still hallucinates |

### Key Technical Findings

1. **MASt3R depth extraction**: pts3d is in world coords, need to compute z-depth along camera view direction
2. **3DGS depth format**: expects **inverse depth** normalized to [0,1] as 16-bit PNG (`invdepthmap = uint16 / 2^16`)
3. **Depth supervision hurts quality** when depth isn't perfectly aligned with COLMAP poses
4. **Narrow walking trajectory** = limited viewing frustum = poor novel view extrapolation

### Core Problem
The dataset is ~4 seconds of forward walking — creates a very narrow viewing frustum. For 3DGS to extrapolate well, need:
- More angular coverage (walking in circle, multiple passes)
- More frames (120 instead of 30)
- Different capture patterns (head turns during walking)

### Next Steps (when session resumes)
- Try MASt3R on all 120 frames for denser reconstruction (~20-30 min)
- Consider whether better capture is the real solution for robotics training data

## 3:30 AM - Frame density experiment results

### Experiment: Training views vs novel view quality

Tested 3DGS with different MASt3R reconstructions:
- **30 frames** (every 4th): PSNR ~26, usable novel views to ~5cm
- **60 frames** (every 2nd): PSNR ~25.5, usable novel views to ~10cm (2x improvement!)
- **120 frames** (all): PSNR ~18.25, training views already blurry — **worse overall**

### Analysis

The 120-frame failure reveals a critical insight: **denser != better for MASt3R**.

MASt3R struggles with all 120 frames because:
1. Adjacent frames (1/30s apart) have nearly identical views
2. Small baselines make stereo matching harder
3. Global optimization has too many soft constraints, converges poorly

**Sweet spot appears to be ~60 frames** (every 2nd frame) for this 4-second walking sequence.

### Updated conclusions

| Frames | PSNR | Novel view limit | Notes |
|--------|------|------------------|-------|
| 30 | 26 | ~5cm | Good reconstruction, limited extrapolation |
| 60 | 25.5 | ~10cm | Best balance for this dataset |
| 120 | 18.25 | N/A | Poor reconstruction, MASt3R fails |

### Practical recommendation

For walking video → 3DGS novel view synthesis:
- **Sample every 2nd-4th frame** (not every frame)
- More frames help extrapolation but only if pose quality stays high
- ~10cm lateral offset is achievable with proper sampling
- For robotics training data: this may be sufficient for close-range manipulation views

### Next steps?
- The 60-frame model is the best we can achieve with this capture geometry
- For larger offsets (>10cm), need fundamentally different capture: head turns, circular walking, multiple passes
- Could explore: depth supervision with 60-frame model, longer training, different 3DGS params

**Conclusion:** ViewCrafter is great for content creation / film VFX, wrong for robotics training data where the model needs to predict what it will *actually* see.
