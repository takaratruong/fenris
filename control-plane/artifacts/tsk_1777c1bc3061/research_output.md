## The CAP Theorem Trade-off

**Interesting Fact:** In distributed systems, the CAP theorem (proven by Eric Brewer in 2000) states that a distributed data store can only provide two of these three guarantees simultaneously:

1. **Consistency** - Every read receives the most recent write or an error
2. **Availability** - Every request receives a response (without guarantee it's the most recent)
3. **Partition tolerance** - The system continues to operate despite network partitions

**Why it's interesting:** During a network partition (which *will* happen in real systems), you must choose between consistency and availability. This fundamental trade-off shapes the design of every distributed database:

- **CP systems** (like ZooKeeper, HBase): Sacrifice availability during partitions to maintain consistency
- **AP systems** (like Cassandra, DynamoDB): Sacrifice consistency during partitions to stay available
